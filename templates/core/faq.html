Frequently Asked Questions

This page answers common questions about how MintKit Hub and the MintKit Studio app fit together - first in simple terms, then (optionally) with more technical details for anyone who is curious.

Getting started

Do my customers need a MintKit account?

Short answer: No. Your customers only see your public storefront and the digital cards or tickets they receive by email. They don't need to register or install an app.

Show technical details

When a customer buys a card or ticket, MintKit creates a unique, secret link for that specific asset. That link is sent by email and can be opened in a normal browser on phone or desktop. Under the hood, the card is backed by a token/NFT on a blockchain, but the customer never has to deal with seed phrases, wallets or exchanges.

What is the difference between MintKit Hub and MintKit Studio?

Short answer: MintKit Hub (this website) is where you manage your account, subscription and storefront. MintKit Studio is a separate app where the actual digital gift cards, tickets and vouchers are created, minted and managed.

Show technical details

MintKit Hub is a classic Web 2 Django site (accounts, forms, Stripe, storefront management). MintKit Studio runs on a blockchain platform and handles the “heavy” cryptographic work - minting tokens, storing card metadata and serving secure QR codes. Hub simply controls who is allowed to open Studio and keeps a record of which business profile is linked to which on-chain identity.

Payments & pricing

How do I pay for MintKit?

Short answer: You subscribe to a plan (after a free trial) using Stripe. Payments are taken in normal currency.

Show technical details

Stripe handles all card details on its own hosted pages. MintKit Hub only stores the minimum Stripe IDs needed to know which plan you are on and whether your subscription is active. Blockchain transaction fees and storage costs are bundled into MintKit's platform fee and are never billed to you separately.

What happens when my free trial ends?

Short answer: You can upgrade to a paid plan to keep full access. If you don't upgrade, your storefront stays in place but access to MintKit Studio and minting tools is limited or paused.

Show technical details

Internally, MintKit Hub stores the start and end date of your trial. Once the trial passes and there is no active subscription, the app will restrict user from editing/creating/minting or managing digital cards. Existing cards remain valid; you simply cannot create new ones or manage existing ones until you re-activate a plan.

Storefronts & customers

Can I hide my storefront while I am still setting things up?

Short answer: Yes. You can keep your storefront inactive so only you can see it. When you are ready you switch it to active and it becomes publicly visible.

What happens when a customer buys a card or ticket?

Short answer: The customer pays through Stripe, and then receives an email containing a secure link and QR code for their digital card or ticket.

Show technical details

Depending on the chosen flow, cards can be pre-minted in batches or minted “on claim” when a customer activates their purchase via email. Either way, each card is represented by a unique on-chain token and linked to a short, human-friendly web page that the customer uses in everyday life.

For the curious & technically minded

Why does MintKit use a blockchain at all?

Short answer: To give your digital cards properties that normal databases can't easily guarantee - uniqueness, tamper-resistance, and long-term verifiability - while still keeping the experience simple for you and your customers.

Show technical details

MintKit Studio runs on a modern smart-contract platform based on the Internet Computer (ICP) blockchain. Your project lives inside isolated smart-contract containers (“canisters”) that are replicated across multiple independent nodes in different data centres. There is no single database or traditional web server that can be accidentally wiped.

Each card or ticket is effectively an NFT or token with structured metadata (value, expiry, status, usage history). Smart-contract logic enforces basic rules such as:

  • only one active owner at a time,
  • limited lifetime or expiry date,
  • single-use or multi-use access, and
  • optional transfer or resale limits (planned feature).

Can tickets be tied to a person, place or time?

Short answer: The current project focuses on simple “show and scan” digital cards. The roadmap includes tighter controls such as name-locking, time windows or location hints for specific card types.

Show technical details

Because the underlying token logic is programmable, future versions can support richer policies, for example:

  • Linking a ticket to an email, phone number or verified identity (so resale is limited or recorded).
  • Time-window access (valid only between specific dates or event times).
  • Geo-hints or venue-bound passes that are intended to be scanned only in certain locations.
  • Rules around resale - for example, a maximum markup, or a limited number of allowed transfers.

These features build on the same cryptographic foundation without changing the basic experience for the end user: they still just tap a link or show a QR code.

How everything fits together (A-Z overview)

Click to see the full end-to-end flow
  1. You register on MintKit Hub and create your profile.
  2. You set up your storefront (name, description, logo, basic text).
  3. You start a trial or subscription via Stripe.
  4. From your dashboard you open MintKit Studio, which knows which business profile and subscription plan you belong to.
  5. In Studio you design your digital products - gift cards, tickets or vouchers - and decide which ones should appear on your storefront.
  6. Customers visit your storefront, follow links to buy, pay via Stripe and receive an email with a secure card/ticket link backed by a token on the blockchain.
  7. When the card is used or scanned, Studio checks the token's status and business rules (valid, expired, already used, transferable, etc.) and responds accordingly.
  8. In future versions, Hub and Studio will exchange more data - for example simple stats such as total cards minted, sold and redeemed - so you can see a high-level view directly on your MintKit Hub dashboard.
