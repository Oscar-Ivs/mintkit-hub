Pricing

MintKit Hub starts with a simple two-step model: a short free trial so you can set everything up, then an affordable Basic plan. A more advanced Pro tier is planned for the future.

Plans at a glance

Free Trial

£0 for 14 days

Platform fee: 10% per paid transaction

  • Full access to MintKit Hub dashboard.
  • Set up your business profile and storefront.
  • Connect Stripe (test mode for this project).
  • Try creating a small set of digital gift cards / tickets / vouchers.
  • Up to 3 collections and roughly 20 cards per batch.

Basic

£6.99 / month

Platform fee: 7.5% per paid transaction

  • Everything in the Free Trial, plus:
  • Live (non-trial) access to MintKit Studio.
  • Higher minting allowance for real customers.
  • Up to 5 collections.
  • Up to 100 active cards in total (for example 20 cards x 5 batches).
  • Email support with reasonable response time.

Stripe subscription flow will live here in the final version.

Pro Coming soon

Planned: £9.99 / month

Planned fee: 5% per paid transaction

  • Higher card limits (for example 250-500 active cards).
  • Larger batch sizes for busy businesses.
  • Priority support and faster response times.
  • Richer analytics on sales and redemptions.
  • Advanced tools such as promo codes and more powerful voucher / access-card options.

Shown for roadmap purposes - not implemented in this project.

What every plan includes

  • One business profile and one primary storefront.
  • Clean public URL for your storefront.
  • Bridge to the external MintKit Studio where cards are designed and minted.
  • Core features focused on digital gift cards, tickets and vouchers.

Notes for this college project

The limits and fees above are mainly to explain how MintKit Hub could work as a real SaaS product. For the Code Institute submission the focus is on:

  • account & profile management,
  • storefront creation and public view,
  • basic subscription structure and trial concept,
  • linking to the external MintKit Studio application.

Some behaviours (such as strict card limits and full Stripe billing logic) are described here for context but are not fully enforced in this version.
